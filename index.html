Glossary

📘 Credit Card Basics

A credit card is a payment card issued by a financial institution that allows you to borrow money up to a pre-set credit limit. When you make a purchase, the bank pays the merchant on your behalf. You then repay the bank — either in full each month or in installments, with interest.

Every month you receive a statement showing your purchases. You have two choices: pay the full balance (no interest charged) or pay the minimum (interest accrues on the remaining balance).

Your credit limit is the maximum amount you're allowed to borrow on a credit card at any one time. For example, if your limit is ₹1,00,000, you cannot make purchases that push your total balance above that amount.

Credit limits are set by the issuer based on your income, credit score, and credit history. You can request an increase after demonstrating responsible usage.

  • Debit card: Spends your own money directly from your bank account.
  • Credit card: Borrows money from the bank; you repay later.
  • Credit building: Credit cards help build credit history; debit cards do not.
  • Rewards: Credit cards typically offer better rewards and protections.
  • Risk: Debit cards expose your actual bank balance if compromised; credit cards offer stronger fraud protection.

A billing cycle is the period (usually 28–31 days) during which all your transactions are recorded. At the end of the cycle, the bank generates your statement showing the total balance owed. You then have a grace period (typically 20–25 days) to pay before interest is charged.

The grace period is the window of time between the end of your billing cycle and your payment due date — typically 21 to 25 days. If you pay your full statement balance before this deadline, you pay zero interest on purchases made during that cycle. Carrying a balance from month to month eliminates the grace period.

📝 Applying for a Credit Card

Typical documents required include:

  • Identity proof: Aadhaar card, PAN card, passport, voter ID
  • Address proof: Utility bill, Aadhaar, rental agreement
  • Income proof: Salary slips (last 3 months), ITR, Form 16, or bank statements
  • Photographs: Recent passport-size photos

Requirements vary by bank and card type. Premium cards may need higher income documentation.

Most banks prefer a CIBIL score of 750 or above for approval on standard cards. Here's a general guide:

  • 750–900: Excellent – easy approval, best offers
  • 700–749: Good – likely approved with standard terms
  • 650–699: Fair – may face higher interest rates or lower limits
  • Below 650: Poor – consider a secured card first

  • Entry-level cards: Many banks offer cards for first-time applicants

🎁 Rewards, Cashback & Points

Every time you spend, you earn points — typically 1 to 5 points per ₹100 spent, depending on the card and category. Accelerated rewards are often available for dining, travel, or online shopping. Points can be redeemed for:

  • Flight tickets and hotel bookings
  • Cashback or statement credits
  • Gift vouchers and merchandise
  • Utility bill payments

Points usually expire in 2–3 years, so redeem them before they lapse.

Cashback means you get a percentage of your spending back — directly credited to your account or adjusted against your bill. For example, a 5% cashback on grocery spending of ₹5,000 gives you ₹250 back. Cashback cards are simpler than points cards — what you earn is what you get, with no conversion complexity.

A welcome bonus is a one-time reward for new cardholders, often triggered by spending a minimum amount within the first few months. Examples: "Earn 10,000 bonus points when you spend ₹30,000 in the first 90 days." Read the fine print carefully — some bonuses require activation or are only available to first-time applicants of the issuer.

Most reward points expire after 2–3 years from the date of earning. Some cards reset expiry with each new transaction. Always check your card's specific policy. To avoid expiry, set a reminder to redeem before the deadline or use points for small redemptions regularly.

  • Cashback cards are best if you want simplicity and guaranteed value — great for everyday spending.
  • Points/miles cards can offer higher value if you actively redeem for flights and hotels — but require more management.

For most casual users, cashback cards are easier. Frequent travelers benefit more from points/miles programs.

💡 Pro Tip: Maximize Your Rewards

Use category-specific cards — one card for dining, another for fuel, another for online shopping. Each card can give you 5–10% on its specialty category, dramatically increasing total rewards without extra spending.

💰 Fees & Charges Explained

An annual fee is a yearly charge for owning the card — ranging from ₹500 to ₹10,000+. Premium cards charge more but offer richer benefits (airport lounges, concierge, high reward rates). Calculate if the benefits you actually use outweigh the fee. Many cards waive the annual fee if you spend a minimum amount per year.

If you miss your payment due date or pay less than the minimum amount, a late payment fee is charged — typically ₹100 to ₹1,300 depending on your outstanding balance. It also negatively impacts your credit score. Always set up auto-pay for at least the minimum due to avoid this.

Using your credit card to withdraw cash from an ATM triggers a cash advance fee — usually 2.5–3% of the amount withdrawn (minimum ₹500). Worse, interest starts immediately with no grace period, at rates of 36–42% per annum. Avoid cash advances except in genuine emergencies.

When you use your card abroad or on international websites, a foreign transaction fee (typically 1.5–3.5% of the transaction value) is charged to cover currency conversion costs. If you travel or shop internationally often, consider a card specifically designed for international use that waives this fee.

If your spending exceeds your credit limit (when over-limit spending is enabled), the bank charges an over-limit fee — usually 2.5% of the excess amount. It's best to set up spending alerts so you never approach your limit accidentally.

📈 Interest Rates & APR

APR (Annual Percentage Rate) is the yearly interest rate charged on balances carried over from month to month. In India, credit card APRs typically range from 24% to 48% per annum. This is very high, which is why paying your full balance every month is crucial to avoiding interest charges entirely.

Interest is calculated on the Average Daily Balance using this formula:

Interest = (Balance × APR) ÷ 365 × Number of Days

Example: If you carry a ₹10,000 balance at 36% APR for 30 days: (10,000 × 0.36) ÷ 365 × 30 = ₹295.89 in interest for just one month.

The minimum payment is usually 5% of the outstanding balance or ₹200–500, whichever is higher. Paying only the minimum:

  • Avoids late fees and protects your credit score
  • But interest compounds on the remaining balance
  • A ₹50,000 balance at 36% APR paying only minimums could take years to repay and cost you far more than the original debt

Always aim to pay more than the minimum — ideally the full balance.

EMI (Equated Monthly Installment) conversion lets you split a large purchase into smaller monthly payments. Banks offer 3, 6, 9, 12, or 24-month EMI options. While it eases cash flow, EMI often comes with a processing fee (1–3%) and interest — check the effective cost before converting. Some cards offer no-cost EMI on partner merchants where the interest is subsidized.

⭐ Credit Score & Credit Health

Credit cards are one of the most powerful tools for building credit. They affect your score through:

  • Payment history (35%): Paying on time is the single biggest factor
  • Credit utilization (30%): Keep usage below 30% of your limit
  • Length of credit history (15%): Older accounts help your score
  • Credit mix (10%): Having a card alongside loans improves diversity
  • New inquiries (10%): Limit applications to avoid multiple hard pulls

Credit utilization is the percentage of your available credit you're using. Formula: (Balance ÷ Credit Limit) × 100. If your limit is ₹1,00,000 and your balance is ₹40,000, your utilization is 40%.

Best practice: Keep utilization below 30% (ideally below 10%) for a healthy credit score. High utilization signals financial stress to lenders.

⚖️ Balance, Payments & Debt

A balance transfer allows you to move debt from a high-interest card to a new card that offers a low or 0% introductory APR for a promotional period (typically 6–18 months). This can save significant interest. Be aware of:

  • Balance transfer fee: usually 1–3% of the amount transferred
  • What the APR becomes after the promotional period ends
  • You must still make minimum payments during the promo period

Always pay in full if possible. Paying the full statement balance by the due date means you pay zero interest and enjoy the float (spending today, paying next month). Installments always cost you more due to interest — the only exception is genuinely 0% no-cost EMI offers from merchants where the cost is subsidized.

If you can't pay the full amount, at minimum pay the minimum due to avoid late fees and credit score damage. If you truly cannot pay anything:

  • Contact your bank immediately — many offer hardship programs
  • Request a temporary interest rate reduction or payment deferment
  • Consider credit counseling to negotiate settlements
  • Avoid ignoring the debt — it leads to collection calls and legal action

🔒 Fraud Prevention & Security

  • Immediately block the card via your bank's app, website, or helpline
  • Report unauthorized transactions to the bank within 3 days
  • File a police FIR for a stolen card
  • Request a replacement card
  • Review your recent transactions for any fraudulent charges

Under RBI guidelines, you are not liable for unauthorized transactions reported promptly. Delays can affect your liability.

Skimming involves a device installed on ATMs or POS terminals that secretly copies your card data. To protect yourself:

  • Use contactless (tap) payments whenever possible
  • Cover the keypad when entering your PIN
  • Inspect ATMs for anything loose or unusual before inserting your card
  • Prefer ATMs inside bank branches over standalone machines
  • Enable SMS/email alerts for every transaction
  • Only shop on websites with HTTPS (padlock icon in browser)
  • Never save your card details on unfamiliar websites
  • Use virtual card numbers for one-time online purchases (available on some cards)
  • Enable 3D Secure / OTP authentication
  • Use a dedicated card with a low limit exclusively for online shopping
  • Avoid shopping on public Wi-Fi

A chargeback is a reversal of a credit card transaction initiated by the bank on your behalf — typically when a merchant fails to deliver goods, charges you incorrectly, or you don't recognize a transaction. To file one:

  • Contact the merchant first for a resolution
  • If unresolved, call your bank or raise a dispute in the app
  • Provide transaction details, screenshots, and communication with the merchant
  • Banks typically investigate within 45–90 days

Types of Credit Cards

Different cards serve different needs — here's a breakdown.

🔒

Secured Credit Card

Backed by a fixed deposit. Ideal for beginners or those rebuilding credit. Credit limit equals FD amount. Converts to unsecured after 12–18 months of good behaviour.

💸

Cashback Card

Earns a percentage of spending back as cash. Best for everyday spenders who want simple, guaranteed value without managing points programs.

✈️

Travel / Miles Card

Earns airline miles or hotel points. Includes perks like airport lounge access, travel insurance, and zero forex fees. Best for frequent flyers.

🛍️

Shopping Card

Accelerated rewards or cashback on e-commerce, retail, or specific merchant partners. Great for online shoppers and deal seekers.

⛽

Fuel Card

Offers surcharge waivers and cashback on fuel purchases. Targeted at drivers with high monthly fuel bills at partner petrol stations.

👑

Premium / Super-Premium Card

High annual fees (₹5,000–₹50,000+) but includes golf club access, concierge, high reward rates, global lounge access, and luxury lifestyle benefits.

🎓

Student Card

Low income threshold, low credit limit. Perfect for college students building their first credit history with minimal risk.

🏢

Business Credit Card

Designed for SMEs and self-employed individuals. Higher limits, expense tracking tools, employee cards, and business-specific rewards on travel, office supplies, and subscriptions.

🤝

Co-branded Card

Issued in partnership with a specific brand (e.g., Amazon, Flipkart, Air India). Offers maximum rewards when used with the partner brand's ecosystem.

✈️ Travel Credit Cards Q&A

Airport lounge access allows cardholders to enter premium airport lounges that offer free food, drinks, Wi-Fi, showers, and a comfortable waiting area. Access is typically provided through:

  • Priority Pass: Global network of 1,300+ lounges
  • DreamFolks / Dreamfolks: India-specific network
  • Direct Tie-ups: With specific airline or airport lounges

Most cards offer 2–8 complimentary visits per year. Additional visits cost ₹2,000–₹2,500 each.

Many premium travel cards include complimentary travel insurance covering:

  • Flight delay/cancellation coverage
  • Lost baggage reimbursement
  • Emergency medical expenses abroad
  • Trip cancellation due to illness

Coverage is typically activated only when you book the travel using that specific card. Always read policy terms and check coverage limits before relying on card insurance alone.

🏢 Business Credit Cards

  • Business cards offer higher credit limits, employee add-on cards, and spending reports by category
  • Rewards on business cards focus on business expenses: travel, office supplies, cloud subscriptions, dining
  • Liability: Business cards may have the owner personally liable; corporate cards may not
  • Tax benefits: Business expenses on a dedicated card are easier to track and claim as deductions

Yes! Most banks issue business credit cards to sole proprietors, freelancers, and self-employed individuals. You'll typically need:

  • GST registration or business registration proof
  • Last 2 years' ITR showing business income
  • Bank account statements (business account preferred)
  • Good personal credit score (since you're personally liable)

✅ Before You Apply – Credit Card Checklist

Use this checklist every time you consider a new credit card:

  • Check your credit score — aim for 750+ for best approvals
  • Calculate your monthly spending across key categories (dining, fuel, online, travel)
  • Compare annual fee vs. the value of benefits you'll actually use
  • Check the interest rate (APR) if you ever carry a balance
  • Read the rewards redemption terms — check expiry and minimum redemption
  • Confirm if there's a welcome bonus and what the spend threshold is
  • Check foreign transaction fees if you travel or shop internationally
  • Understand the credit limit you're likely to receive
  • Set up auto-pay for at least the minimum due before activating
  • Enable transaction alerts via SMS and email immediately on activation

Credit Card Glossary

Key terms every credit card user should know.

APR (Annual Percentage Rate)
The yearly interest rate charged on carried balances. In India, typically 24–48%.
Billing Cycle
The period (usually ~30 days) during which transactions are recorded before a statement is generated.
Cash Advance
Withdrawing cash using your credit card. Carries high fees and immediate interest with no grace period.
Chargeback
A bank-initiated reversal of a transaction, typically due to fraud or merchant disputes.
CIBIL Score
India's primary credit score (300–900). 750+ is considered excellent by most lenders.
Credit Limit
The maximum amount you can borrow on your credit card at any given time.
Credit Utilization
The percentage of your credit limit currently in use. Keeping it below 30% is recommended.
Grace Period
Time between statement date and payment due date (21–25 days) — no interest if paid in full.
Hard Inquiry
A credit check triggered when you apply for credit. Temporarily lowers your score by 5–10 points.
Minimum Payment
The smallest amount you must pay each cycle to avoid late fees. Usually 5% of balance or ₹200–500.
Statement Balance
Total amount owed at the end of your billing cycle as shown on your statement.
Balance Transfer
Moving debt from one card to another, usually to take advantage of a lower promotional interest rate.
No-Cost EMI
An installment option where the interest is subsidized by the merchant, making it effectively interest-free.
Priority Pass
A global membership program providing access to 1,300+ airport lounges worldwide, included with many premium cards.
Secured Card
A credit card backed by a fixed deposit as collateral. Ideal for credit beginners.
Forex Markup Fee
A fee charged on international transactions for currency conversion, typically 1.5–3.5%.
